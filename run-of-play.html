The Run of Play Mobile

Overview

The Run of Play is a prototype for a youth sports league group messaging/social platform mobile application.

The purpose of the app is to provide a space for team participants to communicate, as well as a greater environment for the community to follow and interact with teams. This prototype was designed for an in-class interactivity project, and built on research put together by a former colleague.

I began with the question “how might we improve the youth sports team experience for different stakeholders using available technology?” I then set out to research market opportunities, and address those opportunities with a mobile app.

Audience & User Persona’s

Who's involved in youth sports? What kind of experiences do they have? What aspects do they enjoy, and which yield frustrations? I put together the above user persona cards based off of both research and interviews with people who are involved with various amateur sport teams.

People, Action, Context, Technology (PACT) Analysis

Assuming parents as primary users, their contexts-of-use would be busy, with a degree of stress having just finished work and needing to head to a game or practice. It would be rare if 100% of their attention were captured by the application on game days. It was also important to take into account the fact that I’d be designing for a wide range of individuals, of different ages, sizes, etc.

The current use of technology in youth sports is inconsistent and fragmented. At best, parents may use a team centric app like TeamSnap for organization. There are also league and team data repositories available like pointstreak.com, however their adoption is patchy across sports¹.

Defining the Problem

Research was focused around interviewing parents, players, coaches and fans, and revealed two common pain points:

  1. Constrained, closed communication platforms
  2. Lack of a feeling of community support
Sketching & Ideation

Three main flows were designed for the prototype:

  1. Channel-style group/individual messaging
  2. Public team profiles
  3. Pre-game notification/invite to auto-created game channel
High-Fidelity

Designing the application for an android system, it was practical to follow Google’s Material Design guidelines. The colour scheme defaults to white for new users, but the concept of giving each team the ability to customize their colour scheme was considered.

Group Messaging

Similar to the team communication app Slack, team parents and admin are able to open chat channels based on a topic or theme, and set those channels to public or private. Public channels would appear on the team’s public page, and supporters could also join in on the discussion.

Public Profiles

Giving teams and players a public forum allows for them to develop a better understanding of their league, as well as giving a platform for supporters to share in the teams progress and successes.

Pre-game Notifications

The idea behind habit forming products is to align triggers with the frequency and occurrence of user habits². A concept for doing so would be to automatically open game channels on the day of. The trigger would be a notification send out to parents/players declaring that the channel is open. Upon opening the chat, users will be greeted with a message posting relevant information; for example opposing team stats, among other information.

By regularly inviting users into an automatically created channel, the goal is for them to form a habit based inside the app.
