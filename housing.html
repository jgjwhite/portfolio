Toronto Housing Market Visualized

Real Estate Bubble Concerns

There have been ongoing worries for years in the Canadian media about rising home prices in metropolitan areas like Toronto. Digging into the data¹ shows both points for concern and points for optimism. I visualized my research into a 12x20" printed poster, the design of which was inspired by the work of Nicholas Felton, Mike Bostock and Alberto Cairo.

Demand vs. Supply

Rising home prices are a measure of demand, while home sales and construction starts are both measures of supply (data indexed to the year 2000). By visualizing the data in a multi-variable bar chart, a picture emerges of supply not rising to meet demand.

Mortgage Delinquency Rates

The state of the Canadian housing market is often compared against the American market prior to the great recession ² . An area chart was chosen to compare the mortgage default rates between the two countries, over the same period of 15 years. It is clear that Canadian defaults are stable and far below American ones. This information provides a strong counter point against worries about a bubble.

Shadow Lending in Canada

Four indicators portray a cross-section of the shadow lending market in Canada (the definition of shadow lending is explained in more detail on the printed piece). I tried to balance negative indicators against positive ones. For example, the rapid growth in shadow lending is tempered by the small portion of the market that it occupies. I used a consistent blue to highlight the key information in each chart. The aim was to help the reader quickly apprehend the data, as well as enhance the narrative.

Remaining Questions

This exercise raised just as many questions as it answered. What specific variables are fuelling demand? How sensitive is the housing markets to an interest rate increase? At what point will home price affordability fall enough to prompt a correction? There is a distinct feeling that the growth is being controlled, but the issue of sustainability is still front and center.
