The Run of Play

Overview

The Run of Play is a mobile prototype for a youth sports league team messaging & organization platform.

The purpose of the app is to provide a space for team participants to communicate, as well as a greater environment for the community to follow and interact with teams. This prototype was designed for an in-class interactivity project, and built on research put together by a former colleague.

I began with the question “how might we improve the youth sports team experience for different stakeholders using available technology?” The next step was to research market opportunities, and address those opportunities with a mobile app.

Defining the Problem

Fragmentation of experience across different applications turned out to be one of the core problems revealed through my research. This might look like parents using an app for their children's team schedule, another app for messaging with other parents, and maybe a separate website to view league scores and standings.

Audience & User Persona

Who's involved in youth sports? What kind of experiences do they have? What aspects do they enjoy, and which yield frustrations? The above user persona profiles were put together based off of both research and interviews with people who are involved with various amateur sport teams.

Competitive Analysis

Technology aimed at youth sports teams and leagues is a competitive market. Depicted above are mobile apps from three major competitors. SportsEngine and TeamSnap both take aim at the inter-team organizational aspect, with slightly different takes. For example, SportsEngine also offers a sport specific CMS for teams building their own websites. GameChanger targets fans and coaches alike — acting as a database for game statistics that fans can follow in real time, and coaches can mine for insights. There are also league and team data repositories available like pointstreak.com , however their adoption is patchy across sports¹.

Ideation

Three main flows were designed for the prototype: create new channel based on relevant topics, direct and group messaging, and finally managing public team profiles.

High-Fidelity

Designing the application for an android system, it was practical to follow Google’s Material Design guidelines. Components like cards and list elements were incorporated to divide and highlight different content.

Group Messaging

Team parents and admin are able to open chat channels based on a topic or theme, and set those channels to public or private. Public channels would appear on the team’s public page, and supporters could also join in on the discussion.

Team & Player Profiles

Giving teams and players a public forum allows for them to develop a better understanding of their league, as well as giving a platform for supporters to share in the teams progress and successes.

¹ Pointstreak.com is used primarily for amateur hockey leagues, however similar repositories are not available for other sports, like soccer (in Canada).
