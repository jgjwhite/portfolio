The Port Fish & Chips

Background

The Port is a fish and chips restaurant serving locals and tourists in Little Current, Ontario.

The business partners Steph and Jared were looking to extend their pre-existing branding into a website that can communicate dining information to interested customers. The site would also eventually function as a marketing platform that could help the Port tap into the summer cottager market.

Wireframes & Mockups

After collecting and preparing the site content, a set of responsive wireframes were created to define both the layout, and how different components of the site would respond to different devices.

The high-fidelity mockups went through multiple stages of iterations, with the final versions depicted here. Visually, the hero image is meant to be the focal point, with the remaining content designed clearly and with ample white space — so as to not conflict with the hero. The design was inspired by sites like Firewatch and Create Upstate.

Hero Illustrations

The night and day hero illustrations were designed using a flat design style, similar to the original logo design and branding . The illustration is meant to show off local Manitoulin’s landmarks, like the swing bridge and lighthouse, and also to provide visual appeal to site visitors. In a later iteration, the night hero will activate after hours, and fit into a wider color scheme change.

Iconography

I illustrated a set of icons, once again attempting to extend the brand's visual style forward.

Development

The live website was developed with the help of Zurb's Foundation 6 framework. The multi-layered parallax effect was created by dividing the hero illustration into five layers, and adapting/implementing the code explained here (written by Patryk Zabielski).
