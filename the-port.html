The Port Fish & Chips

Project Background

The Port is a fish & chips restaurant located on Manitoulin Island in Northern Ontario.

The business partners Steph and Jared were looking to extend their pre-existing branding into a website that could serve as a platform for announcements. The site would also function as a marketing platform that could help the Port tap into the summer cottage market.

I designed the content and illustrations for The Port’s responsive website, which is currently in development.

Hero Illustrations

The night and day hero illustrations were designed using a flat design style, similar to the original logo design and branding.

Parallax & Animations

The hero illustration is oriented towards showing off local Manitoulin landmarks, like the swing bridge and lighthouse, and also to provide visual appeal to site visitors. Including a multi-layer parallax (not depicted here) and css animations helped to enhance this appeal.

Extending the Brand

I extended the original branding (depicted on the left) into a new set of weather icons that would adjust to the local weather conditions. The original branding had also utilized the Lobster and Bebas Neue display fonts, however lacked a body font. I chose Avenir to act as the body font — it's wider base and shorter x-heights provided a solid contrast against Bebas Neue.

Materialize Framework

Finally, we decided to use the Materialize Framework during development, and this also influenced the bright color palette selection and use of card elements to showcase UI content. View the mobile web site mock-up here.
